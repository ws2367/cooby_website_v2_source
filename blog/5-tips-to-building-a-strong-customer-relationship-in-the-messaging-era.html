5 tips to building a strong customer relationship in the messaging era

Jenny Kao Marketer
2021/02/28
åˆ†äº«è‡³

Working from home has become the new normal in the post-pandemic era. This phenomena has given an extra boost to the adoption and the use of instant messaging apps. Not only do people use it for private purposes, now using it for work is common as well. It's quick and convenient. This allows us to build strong connections with our customers at a faster pace.

To create a strong customer relationship in the messaging era, the key is to engage with your customers through different channels frequently. Here are five tips to help you build a long-lasting successful relationship:

1. Know more about your customers

In order to nurture a good relationship with your customer, being able to tap into their emotions and psyche is important. In other words, you have to interact with your customers to build rapport. Through the interactions, you will be exposed to their interests, habits and trigger points. And when the time comes, you will have a better picture of what your customer expectations are and better communication for your deliverables.

2. Personalized treatments for your customers

Making your customers feel special is a key part of the equation to building strong relationships. Here are a few tips to do so:

Tailored messaging: With so many chatbots out there these days, you don't want to just copy and paste the same message to all your customers. You want to customize the messages based on their needs to bring actual value to them.

Keep records of all details: After building rapport with your customers, you will know more about them as a person and their preference. Record all these details down in a consolidated place. This way when the holiday comes, simply shoot them a thank you card along with their favorite wine to show your appreciation indicating that you remember the small details about them.

3. Be responsive and don't let customers be in queue.

In a digitized messaging era, no one likes to wait. Customers often expect quick responses from their questions sent through messaging apps. Don't just read it and leave them hanging there without any context. If you are busy at the moment, quickly send them a quick response saying that you will get back to them later. The worst thing you can do is not replying to customers' requests when you promised to do so. To solve this problem, you need to have an organized list, tracking all your tasks each day avoiding any missed requests.

4. Be consistent and connect with customers on regular bases.

All customers care about consistency. They usually don't tolerate neglect or failure to deliver on time. And If you did and they tolerated, you're probably under evaluation for the continuance of the contract.

Engage and connect with customers on regular bases. Don't just engage with them only when you need to sell them something, this leaves a sour taste. Connect with your customers on a monthly basis via messaging apps for a quick update on valuable industry insights or just simply a greeting on how they're doing. And If you are actually working on a project with the customer, give them updates on the progress to keep them at ease.

5. Seeking feedback from customers.

Asking your customers to give you feedback is essential. To maintain a long term relationship with your customer, you have to hear directly from them on what is working and is not. If it's not, having an open discussion about the issue can clarify misunderstanding and align on the same page. When you ask your customers to provide feedback, it shows that you value their opinion and genuinely care about what they have to say.

Building a strong and long-term relationship with customers takes patience and efforts to do so. There are no shortcuts. However, knowing what tools to use and keeping your tasks organized can definitely save you time and energy. Here is a productivity tool that can definitely be a great helper when it comes to building strong customer relationships, you will no longer miss any requests or have all your customer information scattered everywhere!
